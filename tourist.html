River Tourism

From the rugged majesty of the Three Gorges to the neon skyline of Shanghai, the Yangtze offers one of the world's most diverse cultural and natural journeys. For many, a luxury cruise is the defining way to witness the pulse of China.

🚢 Iconic Cruise Routes

Most travelers choose itineraries spanning 4 to 12 days, focusing on the river's most scenic and historic stretches.

  • Chongqing to Yichang (4-5 Days): The most popular "Three Gorges Essence" route, taking you through the heart of the river's dramatic landscapes and past the Three Gorges Dam.
  • Shanghai to Chongqing (11-12 Days): An in-depth panorama crossing the entire length of the river's mid-to-lower reaches, stopping at major cultural hubs like Nanjing and Wuhan.
  • Special Explorations: Premium ships like the Yangzi Explorer now offer exclusive maiden voyages to the Shennongjia Nature Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its rare biodiversity.

🏰 Must-See Landmarks

  • Three Gorges (Qutang, Wu, Xiling): Famed for over a millennium, these gorges remain the river's signature attraction, offering sheer limestone cliffs and mist-shrouded peaks.
  • Fengdu Ghost City: A bizarre and unique 2,000-year-old temple complex on the river's north bank that represents the trials of the underworld.
  • Shibaozhai Pagoda: A 12-story architectural marvel built directly into a cliffside without using a single nail.
  • White Emperor City: Situated at the entrance to Qutang Gorge, this historic site offers the iconic vista featured on the back of the 10 RMB note.

💡 Travel Planning

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring (March–June) and Autumn (September–November) offer the most comfortable weather and clearest views.
  • Modern Comforts: New "smart-space" vessels like the Vista Harmony (launching April 2026) offer all-inclusive luxury retreats with butler service and private balconies.
