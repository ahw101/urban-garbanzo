What is it?

The Yangtze River, or Chang Jiang ("Long River"), is the longest river in Asia and the third longest in the world, spanning approximately 6,300 kilometres. It flows entirely within China, serving as a vital lifeline for over 400 million people—nearly one-third of the country's population.

Starting high in the Tanggula Mountains of the Tibetan Plateau, the river winds through spectacular landscapes, including the world-famous Three Gorges, before emptying into the East China Sea at Shanghai.

Beyond its geographical scale, the Yangtze is an economic powerhouse. Its basin contributes roughly 40% of China's GDP and is home to the Three Gorges Dam, the largest hydroelectric power station on the planet.
