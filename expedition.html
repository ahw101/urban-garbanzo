Expedition

From the first steamboats to navigate its treacherous gorges to modern-day adventurers walking its entire length, the Yangtze has always been the ultimate test of human endurance. These expeditions represent a century of efforts to map, race, and "conquer" Asia's longest river.

🛶 The Great Rafting Race of 1986

A high-stakes international competition to be the first to raft the river's entire length. It became a matter of national pride for China, leading to a tragic loss of life.

  • The Sino-American Team: Led by American rafter Ken Warren, this team possessed world-class equipment but was plagued by internal mutiny and the death of photographer David Shippee from altitude sickness.
  • The Chinese Teams: Two ad hoc teams of volunteers aimed to "conquer" the river for China. Despite limited experience, they used sealed "unflippable" pods to pass through the Tiger Leaping Gorge.
  • The Toll: The race claimed 11 lives in total (10 Chinese rafters and one American), marking it as one of the most dangerous river expeditions in history.

🚶‍♂️ Mission Yangtze: Ash Dykes (2019)

British adventurer Ash Dykes set a world first by becoming the first person to walk the river's entire 4,000-mile length.

  • The Journey: A 352-day trek starting from the Jianggendiru Glacier on the Tibetan Plateau and ending in Shanghai.
  • Extreme Conditions: Dykes faced sub-zero blizzards, altitudes over 5,000 metres, and encounters with local wildlife, including wolf packs and bears.
  • Environmental Focus: The expedition served as a platform for WWF and other groups to highlight plastic pollution and conservation along the basin.
